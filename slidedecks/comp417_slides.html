Overview

Artificial Intelligence is sweeping across the globe. Its pervasive use in society is nearly unavoidable. How is user privacy being protected? Is it too late to set guardrails on Generative AI or is it too far gone, like Social Media’s impact on the world?
While Generative AI (GenAI) is not a new technology, the wide-spread use of it in everyday life is. Generative AI comes out of early machine learning techniques from the 1950s and 60s. It was not until deep learning and complex neural networks were developed in the 2010s. This is what we know as GenAI today.

Thesis

As Generative AI systems like ChatGPT rapidly advance and adapt, urgent international cooperation and legally binding global regulations are imperative within the next 1-2 years to safeguard user privacy and prevent a repeat of the uncontrolled societal disruptions caused by unregulated social media platforms. Without a unified framework to govern the collection, use, and protection of personal data by AI companies and models, the immense potential of Generative AI will be overshadowed by erosions of privacy, security vulnerabilities, and public distrust akin to the Cambridge Analytica scandal.
